FOLK

Traditional music passed down through generations. Usually tells stories from culture, nature, or daily life. Often uses acoustic instruments.

POP

Short for “popular music.” Catchy melodies, easy to sing along. Focuses on rhythm, emotion, and mainstream appeal.

HIP-HOP

Strong rhythm with rap vocals. Often includes personal or social messages. Based on beats, sampling, and lyrical flow.

K-POP

Korean pop music. Mixes dance, singing, rap, and visuals. Known for high production and group performances.

ROCK

Strong guitar, drums, and vocals. Energetic sound, often rebellious or emotional. Many subgenres (punk, alt, indie, etc.).

ALTERNATIVE METAL

Mix of heavy metal and experimental elements. Emotional, dark, and often non-traditional. May include screams or soft/loud contrasts.

HEAVY METAL

Loud, aggressive guitars and drums. Powerful vocals, sometimes rough or operatic. Themes often about strength, pain, or freedom.

SYMPHONIC METAL

Mix of metal and orchestral/classical sounds. Often dramatic, epic, and cinematic. Includes choirs, strings, and powerful vocals.
